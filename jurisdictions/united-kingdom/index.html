Executive Summary

Immigration law in the United Kingdom is the professional legal and administrative function through which foreign nationals obtain lawful entry, stay or settlement in the country for a defined purpose.

Operationally, UK immigration matters often begin with route selection, including work, study, family migration, graduate routes, long residence or another recognised ground for lawful stay.

The UK system combines route-specific visa conditions with increasingly digital evidence of status. In practice, many migrants must manage a UKVI account, access an eVisa, and use share codes to prove their immigration position for employment, renting or comparable legal checks.

Cross-border relevance is substantial because UK immigration planning may interact with multinational employment structures, international education and later settlement or citizenship strategy.
