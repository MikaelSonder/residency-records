Executive Summary

Immigration law in Spain is the professional legal and administrative function through which foreign nationals obtain lawful entry, stay or residence in the country for a defined purpose.

Operationally, Spanish immigration matters often begin with route selection, including work, family migration, studies, long-term stay, EU registration or another recognised ground for lawful residence.

The Spanish system combines nationality-based distinctions, appointment-driven administration and physical status documentation. In practice, some work and residence procedures must be initiated by the employer, EU citizens remaining beyond three months must register, and non-EU residents often progress toward identity-card issuance and local formalities after approval.

Cross-border relevance is substantial because Spain operates within a wider EU and Schengen environment, meaning residence planning may connect to free movement logic, employer structures and future relocation strategy beyond Spain.

2. Route SelectionDetermine whether the correct pathway is work, family migration, studies, long-term stay, EU registration or another recognised route.
3. Filing Responsibility ReviewDetermine whether the route must be initiated by the applicant, employer or another authorised representative.
4. Documentary PreparationGather identity records, sponsor material, employment records, admissions, relationship documents, financial support evidence and route-specific forms as needed.
