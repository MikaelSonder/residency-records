Executive Summary

Immigration law in Austria is the professional legal and administrative function through which foreign nationals obtain lawful entry, stay, settlement or residence in the country for a defined purpose.

Operationally, Austrian immigration matters often begin with route selection, including Red-White-Red Card pathways, Red-White-Red Card plus, family reunification, student residence, EU registration or long-term EU resident positioning.

The Austrian system combines nationality-based distinctions with structured settlement logic. In practice, many third-country nationals planning to remain longer than six months need an appropriate residence title, while EU/EEA/Swiss nationals staying longer than three months must regularise their position through a registration certificate within the applicable period.

Cross-border relevance is significant because immigration planning in Austria may interact with EU mobility, employer sponsorship logic, integration requirements and future long-term residence strategy.

Process Flow

2. Route SelectionDetermine whether the correct pathway is Red-White-Red Card, Red-White-Red Card plus, family reunification, student or researcher residence, EU registration or Long-Term Resident EU.
3. Eligibility ReviewAssess whether the factual background, sponsor structure, means of subsistence, health insurance and accommodation position support the chosen route.
4. Documentary PreparationGather identity records, photographs, employment or family documents, accommodation evidence, health-insurance proof and route-specific supporting material.
5. Filing Location DecisionDetermine whether the application must be filed in person abroad before entry or can be submitted directly in Austria based on the applicable route and entry status.
6. Authority Review and ContinuityRespond to follow-up requests, maintain lawful status and prepare renewal or title transition where required.
