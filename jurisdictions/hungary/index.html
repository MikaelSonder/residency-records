Executive Summary

Immigration law in Hungary is the professional legal and administrative function through which foreign nationals obtain lawful entry, stay, residence permit status or longer-term residence positioning in the country for a defined purpose.

Operationally, Hungarian immigration matters often begin with route selection, including general residence permit logic, family reunification, EEA permanent residence status or longer-term third-country status through the National Residence Card where eligible.

The Hungarian system is procedural and status-sensitive. In practice, nationality, family position, lawful residence history, accommodation position and documentary sufficiency all influence the route selected and the way the file must be prepared.

Cross-border relevance is significant because immigration planning in Hungary may interact with EEA movement, Schengen mobility, employer structures, family mobility and future long-term residence strategy.
