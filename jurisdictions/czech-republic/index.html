Executive Summary

Immigration law in the Czech Republic is the professional legal and administrative function through which foreign nationals obtain lawful entry, stay, long-term residence or permanent residence in the country for a defined purpose.

Operationally, Czech immigration matters often begin with route selection, including long-term visa, long-term residence permit, temporary residence linked to an EU citizen family relationship or later permanent residence positioning.

The Czech system is administrative and category-sensitive. In practice, route selection depends on nationality, family position, purpose of stay, prior status and whether the person is moving toward a stay longer than 90 days.

Cross-border relevance is significant because immigration planning in the Czech Republic may interact with EU movement logic, Schengen mobility, multinational employment structures, family mobility and future permanent residence strategy.

Decision Tree

The decision tree simplifies threshold questions that commonly determine the correct immigration law route. It is presented as a logical workflow so the reader can follow the operational sequence rather than treat each route as an isolated label.

  1. Identify who the applicant is, where the person is located and why residence in the Czech Republic is sought.

  3. Determine whether the route is based on work, study, family relationship, business activity or another lawful basis.

  6. Prepare a coherent file that matches the route selected and maintain continuity after filing and after decision.
