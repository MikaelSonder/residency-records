Executive Summary

Immigration law in Germany is the professional legal and administrative function through which foreign nationals obtain lawful entry, stay or residence in the country for a defined purpose.

Operationally, German immigration matters often begin with route selection, including work, study, family reunification, settlement planning or other recognised grounds for lawful stay under the Residence Act.

The German system combines federal legal structure with local administrative execution. In practice, this means that national rules coexist with municipality-level registration obligations and in-person residence title handling by the competent immigration authority.

Cross-border relevance is substantial because Germany operates within a wider EU and Schengen environment, meaning residence planning may connect to free movement logic, employer structures and future relocation strategy beyond Germany.

Process Flow

The process flow explains how immigration law work usually progresses from route assessment to application outcome and later status maintenance. It matters because immigration law is an operating sequence, not a single form submission.

1. Status MappingIdentify nationality, current location, intended purpose of stay, time horizon and whether the case concerns pre-entry visa logic, post-arrival residence title handling or both.
2. Route SelectionDetermine whether the correct pathway is work, study, family reunification, settlement planning or another recognised route under the Residence Act.
3. Pre-Entry and Local SequencingIdentify whether a residence visa is required before travel and whether local immigration authority handling will be needed after arrival.
4. Documentary PreparationGather identity records, sponsor material, employment records, admissions, relationship documents, accommodation support and financial material as needed.
5. Arrival and RegistrationRegister with the local residents’ registration office within the required timeframe after taking up residence in an apartment.
6. Immigration Authority HandlingAttend the competent immigration authority where required in order to apply for or continue residence title issuance in the form of an electronic residence title.
7. Decision and ContinuityImplement the decision, maintain compliance and prepare extension, renewal or settlement progression where needed.
