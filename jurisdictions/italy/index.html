Executive Summary

Immigration law in Italy is the professional legal and administrative function through which foreign nationals obtain lawful entry, stay or residence in the country for a defined purpose.

Operationally, Italian immigration matters often begin with route selection, including work, family migration, studies, long-term stay or another recognised ground for lawful residence.

The Italian system combines nationality-based distinctions, municipality-level residence registration and a structured non-EU permit process. In practice, non-EU nationals staying more than 90 days must move quickly toward a permesso di soggiorno application, often through a post office kit followed by Questura fingerprinting and document review.

Cross-border relevance is substantial because Italy operates within a wider EU and Schengen environment, meaning residence planning may connect to free movement logic, family mobility and future relocation strategy beyond Italy.
