Executive Summary

Immigration law in Denmark is the professional legal and administrative function through which foreign nationals obtain lawful entry, stay or residence in the country for a defined purpose.

Operationally, Danish immigration matters often begin with route selection, including work, study, family reunification, EU residence documentation or other recognised grounds for lawful stay.

The Danish system is authority-led and document-sensitive, with different public bodies handling different categories of immigration matters and with practical importance placed on the correct permit type, filing route and supporting record.

Cross-border relevance is substantial because Denmark sits within a Nordic, Schengen and wider European mobility environment, meaning residence planning may connect to movement rules, employer structures and future relocation strategy beyond Denmark.

Process Flow

The process flow explains how immigration law work usually progresses from route assessment to application outcome and later status maintenance. It matters because immigration law is an operating sequence, not a single form submission.

1. Status MappingIdentify nationality, current location, intended purpose of stay, time horizon and whether the case falls under third-country, EU/EEA or Nordic movement logic.
2. Route SelectionDetermine whether the correct pathway is employment, family, study, EU residence documentation, long-term residence or another recognised route.
3. Authority AllocationIdentify whether the matter is handled through SIRI, the Danish Immigration Service or another official channel accessed through New to Denmark.
4. Documentary PreparationGather identity records, sponsor material, financial evidence, relationship documents, admissions or employment records as needed.
5. Application FilingSubmit the relevant application through the appropriate administrative channel and complete supporting submission steps such as biometrics where required.
6. Authority ReviewRespond to follow-up questions, additional document requests or procedural clarifications.
7. Decision and ContinuityImplement the decision, maintain compliance and prepare extension, renewal or next-step planning where needed.
