Executive Summary

Immigration law in Norway is the professional legal and administrative function through which foreign nationals obtain lawful entry, stay or residence in the country for a defined purpose.

Operationally, Norwegian immigration matters often begin with route selection, including work, study, family immigration, EEA-based residence or other recognised grounds for lawful stay.

The Norwegian system is authority-led and document-sensitive, with UDI operating as a central institutional actor for people who want information, want to apply, want to extend their stay or need case-related guidance.

Cross-border relevance is substantial because Norway sits within a Nordic, Schengen and EEA mobility environment, meaning residence planning may connect to movement rules, employer structures and future relocation strategy beyond Norway.
